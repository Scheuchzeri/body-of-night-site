Preface

"There's a place I want to take you." - Depression Cherry, Beach House

This body of writing, as it began, was a safeplace for my longing and my grief during a period of personal suffering. I feel, however, that since then, it has developed a life of its own. In earlier times, monks wrote on goat skins. But, since skins were rare and texts tended to differ in importance and legitimacy, they were often overwritten, one skin containing multiple layers of text. These skins, in historical terms, are called palimpsests. This body of writing is not a palimpsest in the strict sense, because the text itself, rather than its container, has been overwritten by me again and again, tranferring it from paper to my computer, writing with pen and keyboard, even recording it. Yet, the metaphor of skin fits the intimate quality of this poem. Also, it feels wrong for me to say: I wrote it. Since I mostly copied it. Nonetheless, I care for it deeply. I would ask you, therefore, you who are holding this body in your hands, to treat it no differently than you would treat your own body: tenderly, maybe in frustration sometimes, but most importantly, with compassion. Thank you. ConradM.
