It is only in the park of this city, I will be able to meet you. Here it will be as if we shook hands, actually touched. My body standing, erect your body before mine. Our names written on each others' lips. Somewhere: The smile of a child, violently turned into blood by airraids. Not just an image removed, the body of night is devastated by searchlights. Even as each drop pummels the pavement, the rain and the - Go! Outside! And shake somebody's hand! Anybody's. I believe in the corporeal, so keep it up and the change! I have said this before: there is no right time. Water the plants in September since anybody might not be anybody at all! It is a sudden language, to be heard only when you speak it. Like cats playing on the brink of a rain drain - in the dead of night - waking you up. Then, a respite. As if storms were coming. I dream you up sitting by a brook somewhere, immediately flooded! and and and Langston Hughes loves the rain! Leave me now. I have done all I could. There seems to be something, within each one of us, which must never be touched. I have done it. I have done all I could. And even though I have tried and tried, and even though we've met in my head so many times - it's always the same memory, afterwards. I could make up different ways for loving you. I could make up, make out, make - do anything! For loving you. Rays of sunlight, made up out of milky ways, travelling throughout yolksacks bursting the membrane, grasshoppers, making their way across a greenleaf suspended in midair, laid low above the wind - with or without faces even! Or tracing our lips, that is by smiling, hanging upright in the air! Holding hands, barefooted, all ways, as long as we walk them together, I could make up for love of dreaming you I could be, and in the dark, between us there is something. It would all come back in an instant, I am sure of it: Asphalt and misty rain and chlor! something like washing powder and the smell! the smell! for the life of me I cannot remember the smell! I can remember a hectic bustling about and the sunshine, a heat - another lifetime... And so I keep on writing. I write against that which "cradled sprouts from the ground / made sunlight". Let us get up and greet the morning, all in blue, uniform - Can we trust its patrol? Even if we cannot understand each other? The night is coming on swiftly like your favorite song. Tenderly you know me, and of course I'm scared because of it! I gently rock you to sleep, your body pressed against mine. The dreams, the longing, the wishing, all this? Could this really be it? Following strange peoples, in strange suits, observing strange rituals, early in the morning. I can remember a bus-stop. I was sure, at that time, that nothing would ever defeat me. I felt confirmed in life's unharnished anxiety for - now I wonder... now I miss the rhythmic heat, my eyes are tired, now I wonder... What is essential? Sleep as an antidote against fleeting life. It felt like reaching attaining what I had desired. Experiencing in dumb incredulity the first breasts not being my mother's of a woman I loved - I remember. The question of day, evaded night - I have to face it instead: Which I once loved, where is that place now? A mouthcushion, the early morning sunlight lying scattered by the windowpane; not unlike a dry twig that breaks with a snap - Could I grasp it within my fingertips? Soft blue thoughts before these pastel walls, you eat me up as tenderly comes on the summer dawn, one dreaming, I yawn yawn yawn Y A W N A wide void throughout which these words keep chasing you. As the birds have, I sung your parting. You are long long gone. Stealing past my curtains, twilight is an opening like flesh. Where night's rough fingers touch day fades between your legs. Preface

This body of writing, as it began, was a safeplace for my longing and my grief during a period of personal suffering. I feel, however, that since then, it has developed a life of its own. In earlier times, monks wrote on goat skins. But, since skins were rare and texts tended to differ in importance and legitimacy, they were often overwritten, one skin containing multiple layers of text. These skins, in historical terms, are called palimpsests. This body of writing is not a palimpsest in the strict sense, because the text itself, rather than its container, has been overwritten by me again and again, tranferring it from paper to my computer, writing with pen and keyboard, even recording it. Yet, the metaphor of skin fits the intimate quality of this poem. Also, it feels wrong for me to say: I wrote it. Since I mostly copied it. Nonetheless, I care for it deeply. I would ask you, therefore, you who are holding this body in your hands, to treat it no differently than you would treat your own body: tenderly, maybe in frustration sometimes, but most importantly, with compassion. ConradM.
